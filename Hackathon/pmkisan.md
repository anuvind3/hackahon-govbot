# SCHEME 1 — PM-KISAN (Farmer Income Support)
# ════════════════════════════════════════

## What is PM-KISAN?

PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi) is a central government
scheme launched in February 2019. It provides income support to small
and marginal landholding farmer families. Eligible families receive
₹6,000 per year, paid in three equal instalments of ₹2,000 every four
months, transferred directly into their bank accounts (DBT).

## PM-KISAN — Who is eligible?

- You must own cultivable land.
- The scheme is for small and marginal farmers holding up to 2 hectares.
- The benefit goes to the family: husband, wife, and minor children.

## PM-KISAN — Who is NOT eligible?

- Farmers owning more than 2 hectares of land.
- Serving or retired government employees.
- Income tax payers.
- Professionals such as doctors, lawyers, and chartered accountants.

## PM-KISAN — Documents needed

- Aadhaar card (mandatory)
- Bank account details (account number and IFSC code)
- Land ownership records
- Proof of citizenship
- Mobile number

## PM-KISAN — How to apply

1. Visit the official PM-KISAN portal (pmkisan.gov.in).
2. Go to "New Farmer Registration".
3. Enter your Aadhaar number and personal, bank, and land details.
4. Submit and note your registration number.
5. You can also apply through your local Common Service Centre (CSC).

# SCHEME 2 — PM Scholarship Scheme (PMSS)
# ════════════════════════════════════════

## What is the PM Scholarship Scheme?

The PM Scholarship Scheme (PMSS) provides financial assistance for
higher education to the wards (children) of ex-servicemen and ex-coast
guard personnel. It supports students pursuing professional degrees.
The scholarship is ₹2,500 per month for boys and ₹3,000 per month for
girls.

## PMSS — Who is eligible?

- You must be a ward of an ex-serviceman or ex-coast guard personnel.
- You need a minimum of 60% marks in Class 12.
- Your annual family income must be below ₹6,00,000.
- You must be pursuing a professional degree such as engineering,
  medical, MBA, or MCA.

## PMSS — Who is NOT eligible?

- Students who are not wards of ex-servicemen/ex-coast guard personnel.
- Students with less than 60% in Class 12.
- Families with annual income of ₹6,00,000 or more.
- Students in non-professional courses.

## PMSS — Documents needed

- Ex-serviceman / ex-coast guard certificate of the parent
- Class 12 marksheet
- Proof of admission to a professional course
- Income certificate
- Bank account details and Aadhaar card

## PMSS — How to apply

1. Apply online through the National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in).
2. Register and fill in your personal, academic, and family details.
3. Upload the required documents.
4. Submit and note your application ID.

# SCHEME 3 — Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY (Health Insurance)
# ════════════════════════════════════════

## What is Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY?

Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana) is a national
health insurance scheme. It provides free health coverage of up to
₹5,00,000 per family per year for treatment at empanelled hospitals.
It is meant for economically weaker families.

## PM-JAY — Who is eligible?

- Economically weaker families with an annual income below ₹5,00,000.
- You must not already be covered by a government health insurance scheme.
- You must not be a government employee (they are covered under CGHS).

## PM-JAY — Who is NOT eligible?

- Government employees (already covered by CGHS).
- People already covered under another government health scheme.
- Families with annual income of ₹5,00,000 or more.

## PM-JAY — What is covered?

- Free treatment up to ₹5,00,000 per family per year.
- Cashless and paperless treatment at empanelled public and private
  hospitals.
- Covers hospitalisation, surgery, diagnostics, and medicines.

## PM-JAY — Documents needed

- Aadhaar card
- Ration card / family ID
- Income certificate
- Mobile number

## PM-JAY — How to apply

1. Check your eligibility on the PM-JAY portal (pmjay.gov.in) or call
   the helpline 14555.
2. Visit a nearby empanelled hospital or Common Service Centre (CSC).
3. Get your Ayushman card generated using your Aadhaar.
4. Use the card for cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals.
